Events Overview

The SiX Research Project is dedicated to enhancing communication and accessibility for individuals with Locked-in Syndrome (LIS). Through collaborative efforts, the project explores digital drawing as a communication tool, involving patients, caregivers, healthcare professionals, researchers, and students.

Activity Plan

Exploratory Phase

Led by Bruno Giesteira, this phase focuses on understanding the needs of individuals with LIS and their caregivers. Key activities include:

  • Observational Fieldwork: Researchers, under the guidance of therapist Filipe Gonçalves, will observe patients in therapeutic settings at APELA (Portuguese Association for Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis), focusing on cognitive, motor, and social interactions.
  • Stakeholder Workshops: These sessions will gather insights from caregivers and therapists involved in LIS care, promoting knowledge exchange.
  • User Interviews: Ethnographic interviews and shadow observations will explore the daily routines and challenges of LIS patients.
  • Preliminary Drawing Activities: Pilot drawing sessions will investigate how drawing can act as a communication medium in immersive digital settings, exploring themes like distant memory and spatial memory.

    • Development and Testing Phase

    This phase, led by Pedro Amado, aims to design and test a prototype system using digital drawing to translate minimal movements into digital commands. Key tasks include:

  • Persona Development: Personas representing LIS users are created to refine design focus.
  • Co-Design Workshops: Stakeholders collaboratively refine the prototypes using an agile methodology, ensuring real-world relevance.
  • Prototype Refinement: The team will develop multiple iterations, focusing on user feedback and usability.
  • Usability Testing: Comprehensive testing with end-users to optimize functionality.

    • Dissemination Phase

    Coordinated by Cláudia Lima, this phase focuses on sharing project results widely:

  • Publications and Conferences: The team will publish findings in international journals and present them at conferences related to health, interaction design, and digital drawing.
  • Online Platform: A dedicated platform will offer open access to project outputs like frameworks, guidelines, and software.
  • Final Seminar: A closing event will showcase findings and provide workshops with graduate students to encourage knowledge transfer.

    • Co-Designing and Testing Wokshops with LIS Patients

    The SiX research project aims to empower individuals with Locked-in Syndrome (LIS) by using digital drawing as a communication tool in Virtual Reality (VR) environments. The project centres around co-designing a user interface and a digital collaborative drawing application specifically for the Meta Quest headset.

    Initial Fieldwork and Prototyping

    The research team will begin by conducting exploratory fieldwork with ALS patients at the Portuguese Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) Association (APELA) rehabilitation center. These initial studies will involve workshops and interviews with patients, caregivers, and therapists to understand the needs and challenges of communication for those with LIS.

    The insights gained will inform the design of the initial prototypes, which will be developed using an agile, co-design methodology. Patients, caregivers, therapists, designers, and developers will work together iteratively to create a system that is both functional and user-friendly.

    Testing in Immersive Environments

    Usability tests will be conducted using Meta Quest VR headsets, allowing participants to experience the drawing application in an immersive environment. Focus groups will be used to gather feedback on the user interface, features, and overall experience.

    These tests will be crucial in refining the system's design and functionality, ensuring it meets the specific needs of people with LIS. Researchers will assess the system's ease of use, effectiveness in facilitating communication, and impact on the users' well-being.
